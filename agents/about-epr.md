# UK Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) for Packaging: Technical Overview

This summary outlines the legislative framework, producer obligations, and administrative structures of the UK’s Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) for packaging scheme, effective from **1 January 2025**.

**Note** this is *not* authorative, just a convenient cached summary - consult upstream sources / product owners as necessary.

### 1. Legislative Framework
*   **Primary Regulation:** *The Producer Responsibility Obligations (Packaging and Packaging Waste) Regulations 2024* (SI 2024 No. 1332).
*   **Transitional Period:** 2024 data was collected under the *Packaging Waste (Data Reporting) Regulations 2023*.
*   **Scope:** Covers all UK nations (England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland).
*   **Reference:** [The Producer Responsibility Obligations (Packaging and Packaging Waste) Regulations 2024](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2024/1332/contents/made)

### 2. Producer Thresholds & Classifications
Obligation is determined by annual turnover and the weight of packaging supplied/imported in the **Calculation Year (Y-2)**.

| Category | Turnover Threshold | Tonnage Threshold | Obligations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Large Producer** | $\ge$ £2 million | $>50$ tonnes | Register, report data (6-monthly), pay disposal/admin fees, meet recycling targets (PRNs/PERNs). |
| **Small Producer** | $\ge$ £1 million | $>25$ tonnes | Register, report data (annually). |
| **Sellers-Only** | $\ge$ £1 million | $>25$ tonnes | Registration and annual data reporting only. |

### 3. Packaging Activities (Producer Classes)
Businesses may have multiple roles for different items:
*   **Brand Owner:** UK-established owners of the brand appearing on filled packaging.
*   **Packer/Filler:** Those filling unbranded packaging or branded packaging for non-UK owners.
*   **Importer/First UK Owner:** UK businesses importing filled packaging or taking ownership of imported filled packaging from non-UK established entities.
*   **Distributor:** Manufacturers or importers of unfilled/unbranded packaging supplied to non-large producers.
*   **Online Marketplace (OMP) Operator:** Operates a platform facilitating non-UK sales into the UK.
*   **Service Provider:** Hires or loans out reusable packaging.

### 4. Reporting Requirements & "Relevant Years"
The scheme uses an overlapping three-year cycle:
*   **Year Y-2 (Calculation Year):** Assessment of tonnage to determine status.
*   **Year Y-1 (Obligation Year):** Collection of granular supply data.
*   **Year Y (Relevant Year):** Financial settlement (fees/PRNs) based on Y-1 data.

**Data Fields Required:**
1.  **Packaging Activity:** Role performed (e.g., Brand Owner).
2.  **Packaging Type:** Household (HH) vs. Non-Household (NHH).
3.  **Packaging Class:** Primary, Secondary, Tertiary, or Shipment.
4.  **Material & Weight:** Category (Glass, Plastic, Aluminium, Steel, Paper/Board, Wood, Fibre-based Composite, Other).

### 5. Financial Framework
*   **Waste Disposal Fees:** Large producers pay 100% of the net cost for local authorities to manage household packaging waste. First invoices (Notice of Liability) are scheduled for **October 2025**.
*   **Administration Fees:** Contributions to the running costs of the Scheme Administrator (PackUK).
*   **Recycling Obligations (PRNs/PERNs):** Large producers must purchase evidence notes to meet material-specific recycling targets (e.g., 74% for Glass in 2025).
*   **Modulated Fees (from 2026):** Fees will be adjusted based on recyclability (RAM rating). Multipliers for "Red" (least recyclable) materials will be 1.2x (2026), 1.6x (2027), and 2.0x (2028).

### 6. Administrative Structure
*   **Scheme Administrator (PackUK):** Sets disposal fees, manages invoicing, and develops the Recyclability Assessment Methodology (RAM).
*   **Regulators:** Environment Agency (England), SEPA (Scotland), NRW (Wales), and NIEA (Northern Ireland) handle registration, compliance monitoring, and enforcement.
*   **Reference:** https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/environment-agency

### 7. Future Milestones
*   **1 October 2025:** Deadline for large producers to register for the 2026 relevant year.
*   **31 March 2026:** Mandatory "Recycle" or "Do Not Recycle" labeling required for most materials.
*   **31 March 2027:** Mandatory labeling for plastic films and flexibles.
*   **October 2025 (Proposed):** Launch of the UK-wide Deposit Return Scheme (DRS) for drink containers.
